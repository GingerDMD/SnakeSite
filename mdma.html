Midomafetamine

AKA: Methylenedioxymethamphetamine, MDMA, Molly, Ecstacy

Background

MDMA is a stimulant/empathogen that releases a massive amount of serotonin in the user's brain. For the duration of the experience, the user will experience incredible energy, euphoria, and empathy, with a heightened appreciation for music and a carefree love of everything around them. In fact, molly has occasionally been described as "pure, chemical joy." It's interesting, then, that MDMA is considered far less addictive than its amphetamine counterparts, which provide far less empathy and euphoria (yet can be used more regularly without neurotoxic consequence).

MDMA is only safe when used very irregularly, with most users recommending about 3 or 4 months between "rolls". What's particularly interesting about this molecule are the extremely potent serotonergic properties produced by that methylenedioxy R group attached to the phenyl ring.
