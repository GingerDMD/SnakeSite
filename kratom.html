Mitragyna speciosa

AKA: Kratom, herbal analgesia

Background

Kratom, aka Mitragyna speciosa, is an evergreen tree in the coffee family found primarily in Thailand and Indonesia. The plant has traditionally been chewed by workers throughout the day to relieve pain and increase energy.

The plant has gained popularity in the United States in recent years with those looking to get off of prescription painkillers and minimize withdrawal symptoms. It also has gained popularity as a cheap, legal high. The alkaloids found within kratom do bind to the u-opioid receptors in the same way as typical opioids, but the few studies that have been done on the subject suggest that they cause drastically less respiratory depression than typical painkillers. A few deaths have been associated with kratom overdose, but in none of these deaths was kratom the sole contributor. This suggests that kratom, when used by itself and at appropriate doses, can act as a safe alternative to traditional pain killers.

The two primarily alkaloids found within kratom that are responsible for the relaxing, euphoric, painkilling affects are mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine. 7-hydroxymitragynine is around 30 times more potent than mitragynine, and 17 times as potent as morphine.
