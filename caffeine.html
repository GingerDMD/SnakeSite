Caffeine

AKA: The only drug that it's socially acceptable to be addicted to

Background

The world's most popular psychoactive compound, consumed by 85% of American adults on a daily basis, usually in the form of coffee. Caffeine is also available in tablets or pills, which has more of a stigma than coffee or tea for whatever reason, but is probably the most economic option overall. This methylxanthine acts as a stimulant, but in a very different way compared to amphetamines and other phenylethylamines. Caffeine blocks the action of adenosine to prevent the onset of drowsiness, but has other mechanisms of action responsible for the jittertiness experienced in larger doses.

While caffeine can be physically addictive and potentially dangerous in large amounts, these dangers are often overblown by overly-concerned Facebook moms who act as if the consumption of multiple energy drinks is guarenteed to induce a heart attack. Their intentions may be noble, but the LD50 for caffeine is around 200 mg per kilogram of body bass, meaning a 110 pound woman would need to consume 10 GRAMS of caffeine, or approximately 70 cups of coffee, to fatally overdose from the substance. That's not to say that nothing bad happens before this amount is reached; consumption of a single gram (or about 5 energy drinks) in individuals with low to moderate tolerance is likely to cause extreme restlessness and anxiety. But ultimately, the amount of caffeine found in coffee, energy drinks, and preworkout formulas is very safe for the majority of people.
